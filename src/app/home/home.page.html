There couldn't have been a more resourceful way for me to spend time during a stagerring 20 hours journey from Boston to Honolulu than to chart out the data models for a chat application system design project.
I watched a few videos and went through a couple articles as I struggled to keep my eyes open during the 9 hours layover at Vegas. I optimized my moring time before I headed out for the day by coding the backend in Python/Flask incrementally despite being walloped by various setbacks around data modelling in Flask. Well I wouldn't be so hard on myself about that, Python has just made a debut on my Github. By the time I got back to Massachusetts, I had some rudimentary functionalities ready and things were looking fine on the data modelling front.
Next step in the process was to lay out a frontend so that people who think of 'Postman' as the mail delivery guy can use my app. I initially coded some grotesque stuff in Angular but eventually switched to Ionic once I learned all the ins and outs of it. What's unprecented for me was what web sockets are and how to integrate them in my application. It took me a while to get my head wrapped around this novel concept and before I knew it, my backend was cluttered with tons of socket emit requests.
After 30 days of relentlessly pouring in the efforts, it all added up and the final product blew me away. I decided not to deploy it to Heroku since their ClearDB MySQL addon is crappy and my application relies heavily on web sockets and MySQL connection. My work can still be accessed on Github.
